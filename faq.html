Frequently Asked Questions

For Teachers

Why should I prefer to use Knit over regular texts?

Knit provides an efficient way for teachers, students, and parents to be on the same page about what's going at school.

Messaging is one-way so teachers share the most important class information, reminders and updates, but there is no chance of unfair or inappropriate back-and-forth communication between student and teacher.

Should I choose Teacher, Parent?

Choose your own personal role.

If you are a Teacher who is also a Parent, choose Teacher. A Teacher role is the only one that can create classes, invite subscribers, and send messages.

If you are a Parent, choose Parent. It is not recommended to subscribe for anyone else. Students who have phones, tablets, or devices of their own should sign up to receive school reminders directly if possible.

If you accidentally select the wrong role, you can contact us at Knit@trumplab.com and we will switch it for you.

Will my subscribers remain intact?

Yes! Unless you don't create a new classroom subscribers will remain intact. For a new classroom you have to pass on the new code.

Why some of my students don't receive messages even though they have subscribed?

This may happen if they have subscribed to a wrong classroom or if there was a typing error while subscribing via SMS.

Why some of my students not asked for their names?

If a student received confirmation of joining your class without being asked for their name, that means they already have an account with us. They will be listed in your Subscriber List with the name they entered the very first time they signed up for Knit.

Where does the feedback go?

The feedback which is being sent comes to us (Knit), not the teachers and we take that feedback into considerations for the further improvement in the product.

Why is there a 140 character limit?

Cell carriers limit SMS messages to 160 characters. We add your Signature to each message, so we use 20 of those. It's tricky to be concise!

Can new subscribers see past messages?

Only the messages sent after the subscription can be seen.

What is a 7 digit code?

It's a unique code, generated when you create a new classroom. This code should be passed on to the parents who will then use it to while subscribing your group.

Can I messages individual parent/student?

As of now messaging individuals is not integrated, but this can be done depending upon the feedbacks and requirements.

What can I send using Knit?

Knit empowers you to send texts and pictures, you can use our Attach feature to attach pictures with your messages.

Where can I find my class code?

When logged into your Knit account, just click on the name of the class over on the right, and you will see your class code above your phone number on the right!

To find your class code in the app, open your left side panel by clicking on the three vertical lines in the upper left. Next, click on the class name under "My classes". You will see the class code at the top just to the right of the class name.

Can parents/students respond to my texts?

No they cannot!

Why I haven't received a message yet?

It's possible that the teacher has not sent any messages yet, as soon as he/she does, you should receive them. Here are some things to double check depending on how you subscribed.

TEXT: If you have successfully subscribed to a class by text (i.e. the code is correct and the teacher's name is accurate), then it's possible the teacher has not sent any messages yet. As soon as he/she does, you should receive them. You can text ' help ' to the Knit support number (+91-9619834032/+91-9967341714) and if you get a reply, you're all set! If not, troubleshoot HERE.

APP: If your app shows "No Messages” or you only see last year's messages, the teacher probably has not sent a message yet. Check your Notifications to ensure Push, SMS or Email is enabled.

Can I merge two accounts?

Unfortunately, we are unable to merge accounts and transfer subscribers from one account to another.
The best way to solve this duplicate account issue is to choose one account to be your sole Knit account and use only this new account when you create new classes. You can then delete your second account.

If you have subscribers in both accounts you have two options. 1. Continue using both accounts to message your current subscribers
Or
2. Choose which account you would like to be your primary account. Take note of all the classes and class codes in your second (non-primary) account. Delete your second (non-primary) account. Login to your primary account and recreate those classes with the same class name and class code. You will now have to have your subscribers resubscribe to the same class code.

How do I unsubscribe from texts?

To be removed from class that you're signed up for, reply ' stop class code ' to 9243000080.

How do I unsubscribe in the app?

Select the class you no longer need to be receiving messages from. Reveal the side navigation panel by tapping the 3 horizontal lines on the Right hand side and tap 'unsubscribe'.

Can I delete previous messages?

You cannot delete messages. This is a safety feature so that there is always a clear record and trail of the messages you have received. As you receive more messages, those old messages in the Feed will be pushed down out of sight.

Why am I still receiving messages even though I have unsubscribed?

If you have texted "Stop" to stop receiving text messages but have still received another one of your teacher's messages, it's possible that the message that was sent out wasn't delivered.
